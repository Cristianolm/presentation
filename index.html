About me

Who am I?

Hello, my name is Cristiano Martins, and I'm years old. I live in Amiais de Baixo, a village near Santarém and I'm doing this website to train my programming skills, share my development in the IT field and finally introduce myself to the world.

How I got here

Before explaining in more detail what bring me to the IT field, let me save your time and advance the answer. The answer is professional motivation in earn more money and flexibility to manage work and life in terms of place to work.

But back again to how I got here. Academically I have a graduation in Geography and Regional Planning and master in Territory Management with specialization in Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems, and some non-curricular internship's related to my academic background. In addition I also did some work during my vacations in non-qualified jobs and I help my father when we need, as an auto painter helper. That said, I realized that my professional ambitions would not be accomplished without technical skills like programming, working with databases, and collaborating with others without having to stay in the same place as them. And this is what led me to start learning programming and other topics in the IT field.

My learning of programming starts as soon I finished my Master's thesis; master that ended on the beginning of the covid 19 pandemic lockdown. I from there to here I never stopped learning about the IT field. In this vas field, my skills have been developed in Web Development and Testing software. And this is the type of content that you will encounter here.

What has this journey brought me?

Throughout this journey as a complete beginner, I learned a lot, either using distance learning, as was the case with classes taught by Codemaster (the company that taught me distance classes in web development) or Udemy courses; but also with MUCH study and effort as a self-taught, both in the search for Youtube channels directed to the programming theme, as in the constant use of Google and sites like Stack Overflow to clarify doubts.

But to be more precise I will indicate below some of the stacks where I feel I have made the most progress as well as the ones where I struggle the most to improve.

Where I improved the most
